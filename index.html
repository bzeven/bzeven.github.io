About Oregon

Nestled in the Pacific Northwest, Oregon is a top destination for nature lovers and cultural explorers. The state boasts diverse landscapes, from the rugged Pacific coastline to the majestic Cascade Range. Coastal towns like Cannon Beach, with its iconic Haystack Rock, and Astoria, rich in maritime history, offer breathtaking views and charming atmospheres. The Oregon Coast Trail invites adventurers to hike dramatic cliffs and serene forests, while the sandy shores are perfect for surfing, tide-pooling, and whale watching.

Oregon's smaller cities and towns each offer unique charm. Eugene, known for its vibrant arts scene and the University of Oregon, features art galleries, theaters, and music festivals. Bend, in Central Oregon, is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with activities like kayaking, rock climbing, and skiing near the Deschutes River and Cascade Mountains. Ashland, home to the Oregon Shakespeare Festival, combines a love for the performing arts with the natural beauty of the Rogue Valley.

Further inland, the Willamette Valley, home to over 500 wineries, attracts wine aficionados with its renowned Pinot Noir and numerous tasting tours. Eastern Oregon's high desert region contrasts with lush greenery, featuring expansive open spaces and the striking Painted Hills. Crater Lake National Park, with its deep blue waters in an ancient volcanic caldera, is a must-see, offering year-round outdoor activities. Whether for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or both, Oregon's diverse landscapes and vibrant communities promise an unforgettable visit.

    Fun Facts

  1. State flag: Oregon is the only state to have a two faced flag. The front shows a coat of arms and the back displays the state animal.
  2. State birthday: Oregon's state birthday is Valentine's Day, February 14.
  3. State nut: Oregon is the only state with an official state nut, the hazelnut, also known as the filbert.
